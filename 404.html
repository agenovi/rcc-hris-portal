AWOL detection — live example

One absence triggers the whole chain automatically. Here is Juan Dela Cruz, today:
  1. Portal pulls attendance and detects 3 consecutive absences — Juan Dela Cruz, Spyder SM North EDSA.
  2. HR and the Sales Coordinator are alerted; a Notice to Explain (NTE) is auto-issued.
  3. Juan is sent a portal link to submit his reason — HR reviews and decides (twin-notice rule).
  4. If confirmed AWOL → status changes to Inactive — AWOL.
  5. The branch's Current Head Count drops by one; the deficiency rises.
  6. An agency opening can be raised to backfill the slot.
The portal automates the paperwork and alerts — HR always makes the final decision.

Note on SM-deployed merchandisers

SM-branch merchandisers clock in on SM's biometrics, uploaded every 15 days — so their absences appear after the next upload, not in real time. The timekeeping screen shows this clearly so nothing looks "missed."
